Front Lawns, Back Yards

Grass? Let's rethink this concept: Plenty of sun, good soil. What if lawns grew food? Imagine your yard, flourishing with abundance. Beautiful, delicious, organically grown, sweet fruits and vegetables!

Local Produce is Scarce

We've reached a point where nutritious fruits and vegetables are a luxury. Fruits and vegetables sold in stores are typically grown in mass-productions, commecial environments. Do you feel comfortable eating fruit sprayed with pesticides? Even organically-labelled produce is not exactly that. Grown on deficient soil. Drenched in chemically-synthesized fertilizers or hormones-rich cows/pig manures. Picked green. Refrigerated for months and transported thousands of miles?!

Lets face it: access to local, high quality produce becomes harder and expensive. Farmers markets are a good alternative, but still a lot of transportation is involved since the farms are not in our neighborhoods. we can do better: Join an expanding community of thousands who use their yards and lawns to grow locally. Enjoy the better tastes, improve your health, save money, provide community work and be a part of the food source revolution!

Creating Abundance

Our vision is to organize, motivate, and create incentives for producing, trading and distributing high-quality, organic foods locally. This is what LocaVille is all about. When you eat better, become healthier and save $$, we feel it was all worth it.

Yes, I want local produce!

Search your local map and communicate with producers about buying, trading or just helping out. Everyone needs a helping hand when trees and gardens are loaded! Offer your help by volunteering or, sending your kids to learn where food really comes from *wink* if you mean business and want to go pro: charge a fee!

I have a garden, ready to share!

Care to share or even monetize the excess of tasty produce you and your family cannot use? Become a producer! On your local area map, post your produce for share, sell or donation. Communicate with other local users who are interested in your produce or wish to help! Survey your area: who-grows-what? Sync with others and plan next year produce.
